## The Math Framework

### Core Calculation

```
Value Pool:
  Global knowledge worker wages: $50-70T/year
  25% displacement = $12.5-17.5T/year

Speculative AI Market Cap:
  $10-12T needs justification (rest already justified by revenue)

Required Revenue:
  At 20x multiple: $500-600B annual revenue needed
  At 30x multiple: $333-400B annual revenue needed

Required Capture Rate:
  $400-600B ÷ $12.5-17.5T = 3-5%

Historical Precedent:
  5-10% capture is normal for tech providers
  3-5% is CONSERVATIVE
```

### The "AGI or Bust" Rebuttal

M.G. Siegler argues markets price in AGI. Our math shows the opposite:
- Only need 3-5% capture rate
- That's below historical norms (5-10%)
- You don't need AGI—normal automation dynamics on a large market

## The Belief/Perception Timing Argument

### Tim's Current Thinking

> "I believe the technology is ready right now. But that's not the only factor that determines what will happen. Implementation will take at least 2-5 years, and during that period, it depends whether market confidence keeps up to facilitate the roll out."

### The Two Timelines

**Timeline 1: Market confidence (1-2 years)**
- Revenue growth continues at 3x+
- Enterprise case studies emerge (Klarna-style)
- Leading indicators signal thesis is working
- → Market maintains faith, companies get runway

**Timeline 2: Actual displacement (4-6 years)**
- Organizations implement AI infrastructure
- Headcount reductions materialize at scale
- Labor market shifts become visible

### Key Insight

The bubble doesn't pop when displacement *happens*—it pops (or doesn't) based on whether the market *believes* displacement will happen. Belief buys the runway needed for reality to catch up.

### But Even If Confidence Fails...

Two critical points:

1. **IP doesn't die in bankruptcy**
   - Microsoft owns 27% of OpenAI + IP rights through 2032 (Section 365(n))
   - Google (14%) and Amazon (~10%) in Anthropic
   - Nortel precedent: Patents sold for $4.5B, technology continued

2. **Bubble pop might ACCELERATE displacement**
   - Dot-com crash: Internet adoption accelerated after
   - 2008: Automation investments increased during recession
   - Mechanism: Cost pressure + cheap stranded infrastructure

## Core Thesis (Refined)

**Displacement is the constant; the bubble is just timing.**

```
Technology ready NOW
        ↓
Implementation needs 2-5 years of runway
        ↓
    ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
    │                                     │
    ▼                                     ▼
Market confidence HOLDS             Market confidence FAILS
    │                                     │
    ▼                                     ▼
Smooth rollout                      Bubble pops
Companies deploy gradually          OpenAI/Anthropic struggle
    │                                     │
    ▼                                     ▼
Displacement (4-6 years)            Microsoft/Google/Amazon acquire IP
                                    + Cost pressure forces automation
                                    + Stranded infrastructure = cheap
                                          │
                                          ▼
                                    ACCELERATED displacement
```

Both paths → displacement. The bubble question is about WHO profits and WHEN, not WHETHER displacement happens.
