## Key Questions Explored

1. Does the math actually support the claim that current AI (not AGI) replacing 25% of knowledge work would justify current valuations?
2. What's the right timeline framing - does displacement need to happen in 2-4 years, or is a longer timeline acceptable?
3. How do we distinguish between "market confidence timeline" and "actual displacement timeline"?

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: The Math Shows a Plausible Path, Not Proof

The calculation:
- Global knowledge worker wages: $50-70T/year
- 25% displacement = $12.5-17.5T/year in labor value
- $19T market cap gains since ChatGPT
- At 20-30x revenue multiples → need $633-950B annual revenue
- Required capture rate: **5-7.6%** of displaced labor value

Current reality:
- AI revenue today: ~$100-150B/year
- At 3x annual growth → reaches justification threshold by 2027

**Critical nuance:** This shows a *plausible path* to justification under optimistic assumptions, not proof that valuations are justified. The argument should be framed as "you don't need AGI for these valuations to potentially make sense."

### Finding 2: The $19T Includes Already-Justified Valuations

Nvidia (~$4.4T) is included in the $19T figure but is justified by actual revenue ($130B) and profits. The truly "speculative" portion (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.) is more like $2-5T.

This actually *helps* the argument - smaller number needs justification from future AI revenue.

### Finding 3: Two Separate Timelines Matter

**Timeline 1: Market confidence (1-2 years)**
- Revenue growth continues at 3x+
- More enterprise case studies (Klarna-style)
- Signals that thesis is working
- → Market maintains faith, bubble doesn't pop

**Timeline 2: Actual displacement (4-6 years)**
- Organizations implement AI infrastructure
- Headcount reductions materialize at scale
- Labor market shifts become visible

**Key insight:** The bubble doesn't pop when displacement *happens* - it pops (or doesn't) based on whether the market *believes* displacement will happen. The actual displacement timeline can be longer (4-6 years) as long as leading indicators stay strong in the near term (1-2 years).

This is analogous to:
- Amazon being unprofitable for years while revenue growth signaled the thesis was working
- Tesla valued on future EV adoption while selling relatively few cars

### Finding 4: Rebuttal to "AGI or Bust" Framing

M.G. Siegler argues the market is pricing in AGI, and anything less means collapse. Our analysis suggests the opposite: even modest displacement of routine knowledge work, with AI companies capturing 5-10% of that value, could justify valuations. The bar is lower than the "AGI or bust" framing suggests.
